Time to kill the '1 remote—all remote' rule!

For years, the standard advice for hybrid teams has been: 'If one person is remote, everyone should be remote.' While well-intentioned, this rule often creates a disjointed experience where the benefits of being in-person are stifled by digital barriers.

Why it's time to move on

Forcing co-located team members to sit in the same office while staring at individual laptop screens just because one teammate is dialing in doesn't solve the problem—it simply moves the problem to a different setting. We need to invest in better room hardware and asynchronous communication rather than rigid rules.
